Technical Education and Training

Computer Science and Web Technologies studies

This page documents my self-directed technical education in computer science and web technologies. My studies are structured and intentionally modeled after a traditional Bachelor of Science curriculum in Computer Science.

This education is non-accredited and independently pursued. It is built using an open-source learning model inspired by initiatives such as the Open Source Society University (OSSU), combining structured coursework, documented reading, hands-on labs, and real-world projects.

I built my learning program by reviewing what accredited computer science degrees typically include and working through those subjects independently. My focus in study has been on systems fundamentals, computer programming, networking and connectivity, Windows and Linux administration, web infrastructure, and cybersecurity practices.

Much of this structure was informed by initiatives such as Open Source Society University (OSSU), along with university course outlines and industry-aligned study paths. The program combines structured reading, hands-on labs, project-based implementation, and written documentation.

As of early 2026, I have completed most of the core curriculum for my technical education. I continue to expand this foundation through deeper systems labs in both Windows and Linux environments, hands-on systems administration work, and ongoing cybersecurity-focused projects. My learning does not end when a curriculum outline is complete. I continue building skill through practice, refinement, documentation, and real-world application.
